01 — About

Numbers were always my first language.

Before data science, I spent years in finance — learning that the most interesting questions usually live in the gap between what the data says and what it means. That habit of asking "but what would actually change if we knew this?" is still how I approach every problem.

My MSc at Liverpool gave me the tools to act on it: supervised and unsupervised learning, statistical modelling, deep learning fundamentals, and a healthy obsession with avoiding data leakage. I'm most at home with Python, scikit-learn, and a Jupyter notebook full of half-finished experiments.

I'm open-minded about sectors — finance feels like home, but I'm equally excited by automotive, healthcare, or anywhere a thoughtful model can replace a gut feeling. What I care about is the quality of the question, not just the cleanness of the code.

02 — Selected work

Projects where the process mattered as much as the result.

Featured · Finance · Python

DCF Valuation — Stock Valuation Tool

A two-stage Discounted Cash Flow model with Monte Carlo simulation, built in Python and deployed as an interactive Streamlit app. Lets users input a ticker and assumptions to generate a probability distribution of intrinsic values rather than a single point estimate — because the uncertainty in a valuation is often more informative than the number itself. Combines finance theory with data engineering to make institutional-grade analysis accessible.

Multi-class classification

Patient Diagnosis — Depression, Chronic Illness, or Both

A leakage-aware comparison of four classifiers on 670 patient records. Two features were silently leaking the label, and naively applying SMOTE before cross-validation would have inflated scores by several percentage points. Gradient Boosting won; honest evaluation won bigger.
